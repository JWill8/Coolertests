Reviewing the Coleman Xtreme 70 quart Cooler

Table of Contents

  • Overview

  • Ice Retention Test

Overview

The Xtreme is a hardsided cooler built by campground-mainstay Coleman. Like most Coleman products, they're build quality is solid enough for the average car camping trip and do what theyre supposed to do. The brand name wont impress your campground neighbors, but for those who care about right-sizing their cooler budget for their needs, Coleman has always provided a good suite of products. That said, spending under $65 doesnt need to mean no performance, however, the Xtreme claims ice retention for 5 days in 90 degree temps. The specific model being used in this test is linked below.

First Impression

The build quality of this cooler is what you'd expect from Coleman: a solid feel with sturdy hard sides that will withstand a drop from waist height. The lid seals tightly, the grab handles are solid, but there are a few noticeable cost cutting measures like plasting hinges that regular usage will test over time. The empty weight is around 12 lbs giving some indication of the insulation value.

Capacity Test

This is a 70 quart cooler with stated capacity for 100 cans. This cannot be debated since 70 quarts is theoretically enough to hold over 180 12 ounce cans if the interior dimensions of a 70 quart container were a perfect cube. 100 cans plus ice is completely achievable using math, but we tested it nonetheless. With 100 cans, we were able to fit approximately 25 pounds of ice, which based on our ice retention test, would keep those 100 cans drinkably cold for 3 days.

Ice Retention Test

Test Overview

We tested this cooler's ability to hold cubed ice under average summer conditions over a 54 hour period, simulating a typical friday morning-to-sunday-afternoon weekend camping trip which we expect to be the typical use for this type of cooler. beginning at noon on the test day. As with all tests we do, the cooler was stocked with cubed ice purchased in bag form, similar to what you'd buy en-route to a weekend trip. The average high temperature each day was approximately 85 degrees, the average low was approximately 65 degrees. The cooler was exposed to a mixture of direct sunlight, shade and some cloud obstructed sunlight over the test period simulating an average campsite, boat deck or other outdoor usage scenario. Exposure to hotter temperatures or more direct sunlight without shade will obviously alter ice retention results.

Test results

The cooler was placed into the test conditions with 20.4 lbs of ice (in cube form) and after 60 hours (Noon on day 1 to 6 pm on day 3) had retained 8.92lbs, translating to an impressive 43% ice retention rate after 3 afternoon's worth of sun exposure. Its important to note that the cooler lid was not opened during this time, which helps ice retention. Real world camping usage would have a lower ice retention due to repeated lid openings, but these results should provide a basis for assessing the Coleman Xtreme.

Comparing results to the manufacturer's claims

The manufacturer claims the cooler will hold ice for 5 days. Assuming the ice-melt rate we observed in our tests holds constant over a 5 day period, and assuming "hold ice for 5 days" means at the end of day 5 you have 1 pound of ice remaining, you'd need to start with approximately 30 pounds of ice. However, considering that the test cooler remained closed for the entire 24 hour test period, real world ice retention will be lower than this since every time a cooler is opened, warm air enters, accelerating ice melt. This means that you'd need to start with something more than 30 lbs. If for the sake of argument, we use 35 pounds, This is approximately 42-45 quarts of ice in terms of volume, roughly 60% of the cooler's interior volume in the 70 quart model

So in order to achieve the stated claim of retaining ice for 5 days, you'll need to dedicate the majority of the interior volume to ice. This probably doesn't leave enough room for 5 days worth of provisions unless those items could be stored outside the cooler and cycled in as-needed. In any case, the claim of 5 days of ice renention is upheld, although not probably practical.

Summary

Despite our statements about the 5 day claim being impractical in real world usage, The Coleman Xtreme will definitely keep enough ice to keep your food and beverages cold for the average Friday-morning-to-Sunday-Afternoon weekend trip. Anything beyond that will likely need ice refill, so if youre going off-grid for 3+ days, you'll need to use block ice or pay for a cooler with more insultation. For the money, this is a well built cooler that holds ice for long enough for most people's uses and is definitely a good value for the money

Key Points

  • Held Ice in the sun for 54 hours, sufficient for weekend trips
  • Capacity for 100 cans + ice - Confirmed by test
  • Durable build quality from a reputable company
